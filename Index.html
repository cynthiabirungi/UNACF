About UNACF

Our Identity

A Fellowship, Not a Denomination

The Uganda North American Christian Fellowship (UNACF) is envisioned as a national, Christian-based leadership alliance that unites Ugandan Christian leaders, pastors, and ministries across North America. It builds upon the foundational vision of the DMV pastoral fellowship, expanding into a broader platform for unity, collaboration, leadership development, and community impact.

UNACF is not a governing authority over churches, but a relational network of co-laborers in Christ, committed to advancing God's Kingdom through shared purpose and mutual support — reflecting the biblical model of unity without uniformity.

"One Faith. One People. One Voice in Christ."

UNACF Founding Declaration

"Strength in Unity, Power in Christ."

UNACF Core Motto

Governance

The Servant Leadership Team

UNACF is governed by a Council of Twelve (12) Servant Leaders — reflecting the biblical model of shared responsibility, not hierarchy.

The number twelve is intentional. In the Bible, twelve often represents unity, shared responsibility, and God's people working together — as with the twelve tribes of Israel and the twelve apostles. In UNACF, no leader is above another. All leaders serve side by side under the leadership of Christ.

Our Direction

Goals & Objectives

Seven strategic goals form the backbone of UNACF's work — each grounded in scripture and oriented toward Kingdom impact across North America.

A

Spiritual Unity & Fellowship

The primary goal of UNACF is to foster genuine unity among pastors and ministries while respecting denominational diversity. This reflects the biblical model of unity without uniformity, where diversity is embraced but mission remains shared.

Promote regular fellowship gatherings for prayer and encouragement
Build strong relationships among leaders
Encourage reconciliation, trust, and mutual honor
Strengthen the collective voice of the Church
B

Pastoral Support & Care

UNACF seeks to ensure that pastors do not walk alone, but are supported spiritually, emotionally, and relationally throughout their ministry journey.

Pastoral care systems for leaders in crisis
Counseling, mentorship, and restoration pathways
Safe spaces for accountability and encouragement
Address burnout and ministry challenges
C

Leadership Development & Capacity Building

A central goal is to raise strong, effective, and spiritually mature leaders who will carry the Church into its next season of impact.

Leadership training programs and workshops
Mentorship networks for emerging leaders
Next-generation Christian leadership development
Knowledge sharing among experienced pastors
D

Collaborative Ministry & Resource Sharing

UNACF promotes partnership over competition among ministries — believing that together we accomplish more for the Kingdom.

Joint programs and ministry initiatives
Share resources, knowledge, and best practices
Support church planting and ministry expansion
Strengthen collective impact through collaboration
E

Community Engagement & Diaspora Support

The Fellowship serves both the Ugandan diaspora and broader communities, meeting practical needs and fostering spiritual belonging.

Support immigrants, families, and students
Mentorship and social support systems
Humanitarian and outreach initiatives
Faith-based community development
F

National Coordination & Representation

UNACF provides a unified national platform that gives Ugandan Christian leadership a collective and recognized voice.

Represent leaders at national forums
Engage with the Ugandan Embassy
Workshops, conferences, and national gatherings
Establish regional and state chapters
G

Advancement of the Gospel

Above all, UNACF exists to advance the mission of Christ — everything we do flows from and returns to this ultimate purpose.

Promote evangelism and discipleship
Support missions and outreach programs
Encourage prayer movements and spiritual revival
Strengthen the Church's influence in society

Implementation Timeline

Phase-by-Phase Rollout

Phase 1 — 2026

Foundation & Launch

Formalize UNACF as a national fellowship. Ratify the founding charter. Convene the Council of Twelve. Host the inaugural Annual National Convention in the DMV area (November 6–8, 2026). Establish national identity and core governance structure.

Charter RatificationCouncil of 12Inaugural ConventionBrand Identity

Phase 2 — 2027

Regional Expansion

Establish regional chapters in the East Coast, Midwest, South, and West Coast. Launch Regional Pastoral Empowerment Summits in each region. Begin systematic leadership training programs and pastoral care networks.

Regional ChaptersPastoral SummitsTraining Programs

Phase 3 — 2028

Programme Development

Launch full leadership development curriculum. Establish mentorship networks for emerging pastors. Develop youth and next-generation leadership conferences. Formalize diaspora support systems and community outreach partnerships.

Curriculum LaunchYouth ConferencesDiaspora Programs

Phase 4 — 2029

National Consolidation

Strengthen UNACF's presence at national forums and in engagement with the Ugandan Embassy. Expand partnerships with para-church organizations. Launch national recognition awards program for impactful ministries.

National ForumsEmbassy EngagementAwards Program

Phase 5 — 2030

Maturity & Sustainability

Achieve full organizational sustainability with diversified funding. Review and renew strategic direction. Celebrate five years of national fellowship with a landmark convention. Assess impact across all seven core goal areas.
